Overview

Launching a new coffee shop
with print that gets noticed

Van Dyke Coffee Roasters was opening a new location inside The Atrium in Irvine, CA — a premium commercial complex at 19100 Tower. They needed print advertising that would drive awareness both inside the building and throughout the surrounding neighborhood, including local apartment complexes.

The challenge was creating multiple flyer variations that worked across very different contexts — lobby displays, bulletin boards, and direct mailboxes — while maintaining a cohesive brand aesthetic that felt premium and inviting.

Print Designs

Four distinct flyer variations created for different placements — each with its own color story while staying true to the Van Dyke brand identity.

Designed for
two audiences

The campaign required thinking about two very different touchpoints — building tenants who would see lobby displays, and apartment residents nearby who would receive flyers in their mailboxes. Each called for a slightly different approach in tone and visual hierarchy.

In-Building Displays
Premium lobby placements inside The Atrium — designed to feel at home in a high-end commercial environment and encourage tenant foot traffic.
Residential Distribution
Promotional flyers distributed to local apartment complexes across the street — warmer, more approachable in tone to convert neighborhood residents into regulars.
Companion Collateral
Also designed Prostyle Lounge promotional materials as part of the same campaign suite — maintaining visual consistency across the building's tenant brands.
